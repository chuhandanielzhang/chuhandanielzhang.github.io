Research: Robotics

My research centers around embodied intelligence in robotic locomotion. Locomotion in complex environments (e.g., rubble, leaf litter, granular media) is essential to mobile engineered systems such as robots. Effective locomotion requires sophisticated control strategies that integrate dynamic modeling, model-based control, and learning-based approaches to interact with terrain heterogeneity and achieve robust, energy-efficient movement.

I am particularly interested in exploring how dynamic modeling enables accurate prediction and control of legged robot behaviors, how model-based control provides theoretical guarantees for stability and performance, and how learning control can adapt and improve robot performance through experience and data.
