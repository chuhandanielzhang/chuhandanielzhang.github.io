Robotics Competition

My Robotics Journey: From VEX to RoboMaster

My passion for robotics ignited in middle school through VEX Robotics Competitions and evolved throughout high school and university with RoboMaster. These events are more than just contests; they're a hands-on fusion of sport, strategy, and engineering.

VEX

VEX Robotics Competition (VRC) is the world's largest competitive robotics program for middle and high school students. Each season presents a new game with unique objectives, requiring teams to think creatively and work collaboratively.

Nothing But Net - Team 9698B (Middle School)

In this season, the game objective is to shoot balls into high-mounted baskets to score points.

I co-founded and captained Team 9698B, guiding four classmates from initial concept to a fully operational robot.

Our Creation & Key Innovations

  • Agile Movement: Built a holonomic drivetrain for superior mobility.
  • Efficient Collection & Launching: Designed a wide roller intake system with dual-flywheel launcher using PID control.
  • Smart Architecture: "Front-intake, front-shooter" design for unique scoring angles.
  • Autonomous Precision: Programmed reliable 15-second autonomous routine.

Tower Takeover - Team 9666A (High School)

In this season, the game objective is to place cubes into scoring zones and towers.

I founded a new robotics club and led Team 9666A, mentoring two beginners through their first build.

Our Robot & Key Features

  • High-Capacity Handling: Tilting tray mechanism holding up to 10 cubes.
  • Articulated Stacking Arm: Parallelogram lift with articulating intake.
  • Driver-Friendly Automation: 'One-touch stacking' macro for consistent high-scoring stacks.
